Short Review

This paper proposed the word vector with statistical information by training only on the nonzero elements in a word-word cooccurrence matrix rather than on the entire sparse matrix or individual context windows in a large corpus.

There are two approach to make word as vector. Matrix factorization is one of them. It utilize low-rank approximations to decompose large matrices that capture statistical information about a corpus. It learns from cooccurrence statistical information. One of other approach is Shallow window-based methods. It learns word representations that aid in making predictions within local context windows like skip-gram.

With cooccurrence probabilities, assume that I = ice, j = stream. For words k related to ice but no stream. Say k is solid, we expect the ratio will be large. If k is gas, it will be small.

But F need only the diff of the two targets words, and to avoid being complicated function, dot product of the arguments.   

The thing is that word vector X should be symmetric. So this paper use homomorphism to model it by using such as exp function.

For rare words, it give small weight for them.
